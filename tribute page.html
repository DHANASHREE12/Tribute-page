Dr.Verghese Kurien

Milkman of India

Kurien, Patel, and Dalaya at their dairy plant at Anand in 1970

Here's a time line of Dr.Verghese Kurien

  • 26 November 1921 : Born in Kozhikode, Kerala, to a Syrian Anglican Christian family.
  • 1940: He joined Loyola College (an affiliated college of the University of Madras) at the age of 14, graduated in physics
  • 1943: He received a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering from the College of Engineering, Guindy which at that time was also part of the University of Madras,
  • 1946:He wanted to join the army as an engineer, but his mother persuaded him to join the Tata Steel Technical Institute, Jamshedpur, on a recommendation by his uncle, who was a director with the Tatas, and from where he graduated
  • 1948:He earned his Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering
  • 1949:The Government of India deputed him to a creamery at Anand, in Gujarat where he was supposed to serve five years as an officer of the Dairy division. He personally had no interest in dairy, but had to perform this job in return for the government scholarship.
  • 1962:During the Indo-China war, the government relied on Kurien to provide supplies to the army.
  • 1965:Prime Minister, Lal Bahadur Shastri created the National Dairy Development Board (NDDB) to expand the cooperative program to all corners of the country. Kurien was named Chairman of the organization.
  • 1973:In order to better manage the cooperative body, the Gujarat Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation Ltd. (GCMMF) was set up
  • 1979:He helped to found the Institute of Rural Management, Anand (IRMA) which was based on the philosophy that effective rural development is possible only through professional management.
  • 1982:Pakistan invited him to set up dairy cooperatives, where he led a World Bank mission.
  • 1989:China implemented its own Operation Flood-like programme with the help of Kurien and the World Food Programme.
  • 1990 :He lobbied and fought hard to keep multinational companies from entering the dairy business even as the country opened up all its other markets to them after decades of protection
  • 1997:Former Prime Minister Narasimha Rao sought his help to set up a dairy cooperative in neighbouring Sri Lanka which was done as a collaboration with NDDB
  • 1998:he persuaded former Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee to appoint Dr. Amrita Patel as his successor at NDDB
  • 2006:He resigned from the position of GCMMF Chairman
  • 9 september 2012:Kurien died from an illness at the age of 90 on 9 September 2012 at a Nadiad hospital near Anand.
  • 2013: Amar Chitra Katha published the comic book Verghese Kurien: The Man with the Billion Litre idea
  • 2014 :All the major dairy groups in the country, along with the Indian Dairy Association, resolved to observe Kurien's birthday, 26 November, as National Milk Day
